What is Alex?

ALEX is a 10 step essay writing tool that will guide you through the essay planning and writing process. You’ll start with a blank page and work through the steps to the final draft of your essay. You’ll be encouraged to spend time thinking, reading and planning the structure of your essay. Better planning will immediately improve the content, structure and flow of your essays. With these ingredients in place your writing will be more authoritative, critical, well-informed and persuasive.

No more staring at the blank page not knowing where to start or getting muddled with how it all fits together! ALEX will help you decide the content and structure of every paragraph before you write.

ALEX incorporates some of the benefits of mind mapping/brainstorming but goes a stage further by helping you formulate an entire essay structure. You’ll be able to see at a glance what the balance and relevance of your research is and you’ll be encouraged to filter this material. You’ll organise your material into critical points and those points into paragraphs using a tool called IDEA. You’ll even decide the order of those paragraphs before you write.

In each step you will find an introduction to what the step is about; tasks to perform and further reading (examples or additional information). You’ll move through some steps quickly and others much more slowly. When you have completed the step, just tick the box and move on to the next one.

Comments on their essay feedback often looked a bit like this:

  • Poor structure; the essay feels as though it jumps around without much cohesiveness or flow.
  • The essay has content that is not relevant to the set task/s.
  • The student has not understood the requirements of the assessment criteria or has failed to understand the scope of the essay question.
  • The essay feels rushed with a number of mistakes.
  • The essay contains either not enough references; references that are not varied enough given the literature available; or they do not come from reliable sources.
  • There is a general lack of critical analysis and/or the absence of a through line or argument.
  • The essay feels unbalanced; a disproportionate amount of attention is given to one or more of the themes whilst others are neglected.
  • Paragraphs are too long, or too short, and often do not contain a point – or contain several which are not fully explored.

What do I need to get started?
  • 4-6 highlighter pens in different colours.
  • A pencil and an eraser.
  • A roll of brown paper to draw your plan on. A3 paper is also fine.
  • A magazine folder (an empty cereal packet with the top cut off diagonally works just as well)
  • A shelf or an area where you can stand up your books.
