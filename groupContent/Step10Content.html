When you have completed your first draft you should take a break. Put it away for a day, more if you have the time. When you come back to it you’ll have a fresh eye and you will be able to spot mistakes.

Students are often surprised to hear how many drafts writing typically goes through before it is considered ‘edited’. My last novel, Before I Knew Him, went through 15 drafts. For an essay I generally advise that it needs to go through a minimum of four drafts. Drafting can be split into three levels of editing.

STRUCTURE AND CONTENT

The first redraft looks at the overall shape and structure. It considers content – such as does your argument work and are your points arranged in an order that presents a logical line of reasoning? Are there gaps, questions raised but not answered? Holes in your critical analysis? Evidence not fully explored? Do you make a point without underpinning it with evidence from the literature? Do you make several points in one paragraph when it would be better to split them? You should revisit your introduction and conclusion and redraft these. Do you state your argument clearly in your introduction? Does the essay go on to do what you say you are going to do? Does your conclusion summarise your main points and identify areas where further research would be useful?

FLOW AND STYLE

The second redraft looks at how you say it. It checks your choice of language, spots colloquial language or sentences that might be littered with overly complicated language. It makes sure you use precision – not ‘a number of people’ but how many? It revisits that opening/context statement of your essay, can it be more engaging, more inviting for your reader? It looks at the flow of paragraphs – can you use signposting sentences and paragraph linking phrases to improve the flow? Read this draft aloud so you can ‘hear’ your style, the ear often catches what the eye misses.

NUTS AND BOLTS

Check your referencing. Check for spelling mistakes and repetitions e.g how many times have you used the word ‘furthermore’ or ‘moreover’. Do you start several of your paragraphs in the same way? Have some of your sentences got overly long? Read this draft aloud too. Get someone else to read it. Do they follow your argument? Are they convinced?
