TIPS
  • Take down each of the colours from your shelf and write a first draft following the order of points you have identified as the most logical. Remember each paragraph has a structure – IDEA – to help you draft out your points fully.
  • You can do one colour at a time, taking a break inbetween, or if you feel it is flowing well then do all the colours and complete your first draft in this sitting.
  • Remember: first drafts don’t have to be perfect, they just have to be written. Allow yourself to write in non-academic language, allow yourself to stumble around over what you want to say and how you say it. The most important thing is to KEEP WRITING. So flick that little guy off your shoulder that says this is not of a high enough standard, you are not academic enough, or what you are writing doesn’t make sense. Allow this draft to be full of mistakes – keep moving forward. That means you don’t edit in this draft, you just write.
  • Often, if you allow yourself to write freely, you will think of other points to include as you go. This is not something to worry about, in fact, it’s great! It means your subconscious mind is making connections and continuing to ask questions of your material. If a new point does surface, just find an order for it on your plan and include it.
  • Trust yourself that you will be able to revise your essay several times in order to make correct mistakes, improve the language, and make it flow better.
