ABOUT THIS STEP
xxxxxx by Dr Anna Barker. Length: xxxx minutes

This step encourages you to ‘check-in’ with your assessment criteria to make sure you have not veered off track. The wide reading you have recorded on your Structure Map should now be looked at more closely.

What do you see when you look at your map? Are there lots of points/references under one theme, but not much under another? Is the same book or journal title cropping up too many times, perhaps indicating a lack of variety in your sources? This quick review of how your reading is going may well result in you undertaking some additional wide reading. It’s an opportunity for you to refocus and direct your reading time more effectively and productively.

You should have also gained a general sense of what those ‘voices’ from the literature are saying. Is there one perspective that keeps surfacing? Do you have one study, for example, that presents one way of looking at the issue, but there might be another that approaches the topic from an alternative viewpoint? Be aware of differing views surfacing in the literature and, as part of this refocus, seek out and explore these further.
